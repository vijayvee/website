Vijay Veerabadran

Hello there! My name is Vijay, and I am a Ph.D. student at the UC San Diego. My research lies in the intersection of primate vision and machine vision. I am working to developing the next generation of robust computer vision models by taking inspiration from computational principles that underlie the primate visual cortex. Closing the loop of neuroscience for machine learning, my work also involves the application of state-of-the-art machine learning techniques to analyze neural data. I primarily work with my advisor, Dr. Virginia de Sa at UCSD Cognitive Science, and I actively collaborate with Dr. Terrence Sejnowski at the Salk Institute.

Prior to starting my Ph.D., I spent a wonderful year working at Brown University with Dr. Thomas Serre. In April 2017, I graduated with a bachelors degree in Computer Science and Engineering from
SSN College of Engineering, Chennai, India.

Research

My recent work is centered around the development of neurally inspired recurrent architectures with the help of deep learning, computer vision and optogenetics data. I am interested in computationally reverse-engineering the visual cortex, and also hope to utilize the same models for relevant computer vision tasks with greater robustness and sample efficiency.

Automated continuous behavioral monitoring and traditional behavioral testing reveal early phenotypes in a novel SOD1-G85R knock-in mouse model of ALS
*L. A. MADIGAN, J. PAGE, V. VEERABADRAN, T. SHARMA, J. DOMINOV, T. SERRE, R. H. BROWN, J. R. FALLON
Society for Neuroscience 2018 (Poster Presentation),
Abstract

In this poster, we discuss the identification of early behavioral deficits in the SOD1-G85R mouse model for ALS. We also briefly discuss our automated behavioral monitoring system and its application to efficiently recording and analyzing activities of mice on a massively parallel scale.
